## Task 9

Our second project emitted a different vibe, as we were not able to test each other's projects in person and talk to the developers behind them and give them compliments. I still had a great time browsing the projects of my peers that were posted on Discord, even the ones from people that were not in my section. 

> Project One: https://philippe-bn.github.io/cart263/projects/project-2/ 

To start off, the first project that I found super cool was Escapism. The project felt like an immersive experience with very appealing visuals. For the visuals, I like the abstract world that is presented to us in the middle of the mountains, surrounded with rivers and interesting flying creatures. The color palette used to create this world is also very nice. For the technical portion, I enjoyed the use of 3D, as it created depth and was able to look very far into this surrealist world. The use of lighting to put into perspective and help with putting depth between the mountains was a great move. Also, the lights used to portray the sun/sunset were well done. The movement of the flying creatures was also amazing, as the wing movements were proportional, as well as  different when looking horizontally and when looking up into the sky. 

> Project Two: https://seangregoryv8.github.io/cart263/projects/2/ 

Secondly, the project called Be not afraid, child was interesting to interact with. I'm not quite sure what I am looking at, or the meaning of the spinning gadget-like object in the middle with a bunch of spinning rings that has eyes attached to it, but I like the abstractness of it. The phrases that appear on the canvas might be a biblical verse, from what I read on Discord. I like the visuals and the motions of the golden rings around the eye that is at the center. For the technical aspects, I enjoyed the orbit control where I am able to move around the camera and see from all the dimensions the object and be able to observe it from multiple angles. The lighting also is well done, as it illuminates properly the surface and the parts of this abstract object. I'm interested to know more about this project, as I'm sure there has to be more meaning and references to it that I did not fully grasp. 

> Project Three: https://xiaxueyi00-eng.github.io/cart263-Project2/Xueyi-Xia/html/index.html 

Lastly, this last project called EARTH SHARED SEEN SUBMIT UPLOAD WORLD is very different from the previous ones that I looked into. This project was composed of visuals, as well as a mini game. A lot of detail and care were put into this project, as there are six variations of worlds that we can get in. It's nice that there is a mix of visuals and user interactions. My favorite variation is the “seen” variation with the lamp-like ellipse that would reveal spiders under it. It had a mix of user interaction and visually pleasing imagery. For the technical components, the fact that we can travel through the different variations was well programmed, as well as the elements from THREE.js such as orbit control, lighting, and camera movements, which are well used in this project.

Overall, the projects seen were incredible, and I love seeing other people’s work, as it gives tons of inspiration, and you get to see each person’s creative taste.
